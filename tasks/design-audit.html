What's Working Well
Baseline quality before diving into improvements
🎨
Color system is solid
Sage, terracotta, ocean — these are evocative of Hawaii and work well together. The palette feels authentic.
📱
Mobile sticky CTA is smart
Fixed bottom bar on mobile is the right call. Keeps the booking action visible without cluttering the content.
⚡
Tier differentiation
Featured gold border + crown badge is tasteful. The hierarchy of free → premium → featured is clear without being obnoxious.
Core issue: The design is functional but misses the warmth and trust-building needed for a wellness marketplace. Visitors choosing a practitioner need to feel a human connection quickly. The current layout prioritizes information density over emotional resonance.

Full Priority List
Ranked by impact × effort. Start at the top.
Add bio snippet to compact directory card
Even 1 line of bio dramatically improves trust before clicking. Providers with no bio should show their top modality as a fallback tagline.
Easy · 15 min
Color-code modality pills by category
Body/somatic = sage, mind/movement = ocean, mental/emotional = terracotta. Adds visual scan speed and brand polish. Define ~4-5 category → color mappings in a constant.
Easy · 30 min
Replace services dot-list with pill chips on profile page
Change the grid of dot-prefixed items in "Services & Modalities" to flex-wrap pill chips. Matches the card style, handles long names better, and feels more modern.
Easy · 20 min
Fix the "Claim this listing" icon (Flag → Store/UserPlus)
The Flag icon is universally understood as "report a problem." Switching to Store, Building2, or UserPlus from lucide-react removes the confusion. Also make the button filled primary color.
Easy · 5 min
Increase compact card avatar from 56px → 72px
Bigger avatar = more human connection. The current 56px is too small to distinguish faces. 72px is the sweet spot for a compact layout.
Easy · 2 min
Add star ratings to testimonial cards
Testimonials without star ratings look like unverified text. Adding ★★★★★ (hardcoded 5-star since these are curated) dramatically increases perceived credibility. Also: white card + border instead of gray background.
Easy · 15 min
Show location + key modalities in profile header
Currently visitors have to scroll down to find where the practitioner is located. Add location and top 2 modalities as badges in the header, visible immediately. Zero-scroll orientation.
Easy · 20 min
Make contact items in sidebar visually clickable
Phone, email, and website in the sidebar are all links, but styled as plain muted text. Switching to primary-colored, underline-on-hover links increases tap/click rate significantly on mobile.
Easy · 10 min
Add island-specific default cover images
For profiles without a cover image, use a curated set of 4 Hawaii landscape photos (one per island). Even a simple lush forest or ocean shot transforms the page from "blank" to "atmospheric." Can be a static import per island.
Medium · 1 hr
Embed real Leaflet map in sidebar (replace placeholder)
The current h-40 ocean-light block adds no value. When lat/lng is available, render a real Leaflet map — 3 lines of code. When not available, show a static Google Maps screenshot or a styled area placeholder with the city name, not a blank color block.
Medium · 2 hr
Unify avatar shape (cards vs. profile page)
Cards use rounded-full (circle), profile page uses rounded-xl (square). Pick one — circles are more standard for people photos. This is a minor consistency issue.
Easy · 5 min
Add "Read more" collapse for long bios on profile
Long bios push everything else down. Truncate to ~3 lines with a "Read more" toggle. This is a nice-to-have once the above are done.
Medium · 45 min
⚡ Quick Wins — Do These First (under 1 hour total)
  1. Bio snippet on compact card — 1 line, falls back to first modality
  2. Replace Flag with Store icon in the claim card + make button filled
  3. Bump avatar from h-16 w-16 to h-[72px] w-[72px] in compact card
  4. Switch services dot-list to flex-wrap chips (copy pill style from ProviderCard)
  5. Add ★★★★★ and white-card styling to testimonials
  6. Style sidebar contact links as primary-colored clickable links
