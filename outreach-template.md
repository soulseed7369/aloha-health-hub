# Hawaiʻi Wellness Outreach Strategy

## Why Inbound Links Matter

Search engines like Google use links as "votes of confidence" — when another website links to hawaiiwellness.net, Google sees it as a signal that your site is trusted and relevant. More quality links from reputable sites (tourism boards, wellness directories, local media) means Google ranks you higher in search results for wellness-related queries. This drives more organic traffic without paying for ads.

---

## Target List: Where to Get Links

### 1. Hawaii Tourism Sites & Visitor Bureaus
- Hawaii Tourism Authority (HTA) – state-level tourism
- Big Island Visitors Bureau, Maui Visitors Bureau, Oahu Visitors Association, Kauai Visitors Bureau
- Local destination sites (e.g., gohawaii.com subdomains, visitmaui.com, etc.)
- Hotel concierge guides and wellness packages

**Why:** High domain authority; travelers actively seeking wellness activities

### 2. Local Wellness, Yoga & Health Blogs
- Island-specific wellness blogs (e.g., "Maui Wellness Hub," "Oahu Health Living")
- Yoga studio websites with class directories or teacher spotlights
- Holistic health practitioners' personal blogs
- Nutrition and fitness coaches' resources pages

**Why:** Audience is already interested in wellness; natural fit for guest posts

### 3. Island Chambers of Commerce
- **Big Island:** Chamber of Commerce Hawaii (Hilo & Kona chapters)
- **Maui:** Maui Chamber of Commerce
- **Oahu:** Chamber of Commerce Hawaii (Honolulu)
- **Kauai:** Kauai Chamber of Commerce

**Why:** Local business credibility; they actively maintain business directories

### 4. National Wellness Directories
- Google Business Profile (free, essential)
- Yelp
- Healthgrades
- Mindbody (yoga, fitness, wellness class directory)
- The Wellness Directory (wellness.com)
- Care.com Wellness section

**Why:** High visibility, high domain authority; people actively search these for providers

### 5. Local News & Media
- **Statewide:** Honolulu Star-Advertiser, Hawaii Tribune-Herald
- **Maui:** Maui News
- **Big Island:** West Hawaii Today
- **Kauai:** The Garden Island
- Local lifestyle magazines (Pacific Business News, Honolulu Magazine)

**Why:** Journalists and editors often link to resources; great for press coverage

### 6. Wellness Event Organizers & Conferences
- Hawaii yoga festivals and wellness summits
- Local farmers market and community event organizers
- Spiritual retreat centers
- Fitness expo organizers

**Why:** They often maintain resource guides; event attendees discover providers

## Quick Wins: Free Self-Submissions

Start here — these are all free and take 30 minutes to set up:

1. **Google Business Profile** — Essential. Claim your listing and optimize for keywords like "wellness directory Hawaii," "holistic health resources," etc.

2. **Yelp** — Claim your business page if it already exists; if not, request one. Yelp has high domain authority.

3. **Mindbody** — Popular with yoga, fitness, and wellness studios. Add your listing.

4. **Healthgrades** — Great for practitioners offering health-related services (therapists, coaches, etc.).

5. **DMoz-style directories** — Sites like The Wellness Directory, TherapyDen, and other free wellness listing sites (search "add your listing" or "submit your business").

6. **Local Facebook & Instagram** — Not a link, but creates social signals. Join island-specific Facebook groups (Maui Wellness, Oahu Health & Fitness, etc.) and share your resource.

## Outreach Tips: Dos & Don'ts

### ✅ DO:
- **Personalize every email** — reference something specific from their site or blog
- **Keep it short** — 150–250 words max
- **Lead with their benefit** — explain why linking to you helps *their* audience
- **Use plain language** — avoid SEO jargon; talk like a human
- **Follow up once** — if no reply in 2 weeks, send one friendly follow-up. Then move on.
- **Be authentic** — use "aloha" naturally (not forced); let your genuine mission show

### ❌ DON'T:
- **Mass email** — copy-paste template to 50 sites. Take time to personalize.
- **Ask for links directly** — frame it as "partnership" or "mutually beneficial resource"
- **Be salesy** — this isn't a sales pitch; it's a relationship-building ask
- **Follow up aggressively** — one polite follow-up, then respect their decision
- **Spam comment sections** — never leave "check out my site" comments
- **Ignore their guidelines** — read their submission/contact page first; follow instructions
