## Revised Assessment

### Directory Subscription Pricing: Verdict

**Your pricing is well-positioned for launch.** Here's why:

**Against national competitors:** Psychology Today charges $29.95/mo for a flat-tier listing with no premium features. TherapyDen charges $30/mo premium. Your Practitioner Premium at $49/mo (or $29 Kamaʻāina) offers significantly more — analytics, booking embed, photo gallery, testimonials — and is Hawaiʻi-specific, which means less noise and higher relevance for the practitioner.

**Against full platforms:** Mindbody charges $139–$700+/mo but is a full booking/POS/management platform, not a directory. WellnessLiving is $59–$259/mo. You're not competing with these — you're a discovery and visibility tool, not a practice management system. Your pricing correctly reflects that positioning.

**The free-to-premium gap is well-designed.** A 250-character bio with no testimonials, no social links, no booking, and no analytics is a legitimately limited listing. It's enough to exist in the directory, but not enough to convert browsers into clients. That's exactly the right tension — the free tier proves you exist; Premium proves you're worth booking.

**The Featured tier has real scarcity value.** Five spots per island is a genuine constraint, especially as the directory grows. $129/mo for homepage rotation and top placement on a niche, high-intent directory is reasonable — a single new client covers months of the subscription.

**The center premium over practitioners is justified.** Centers have higher revenue per client, more staff, and bigger marketing budgets. $79/mo vs $49/mo for Premium and $199 vs $129 for Featured is a sensible 60–65% markup.

### What I'd Reconsider

**1. The jump from Free to Premium is steep in perceived value, but the Kamaʻāina rate softens it well.**

$49/mo feels like a commitment for a solo Lomilomi practitioner in Pāhoa. But $29/mo Kamaʻāina rate is in the "cost of a lunch" zone — easy to justify. The question is how long you offer that rate (more on this below).

**2. Consider adding an annual option at launch.**

SaaS businesses with strong annual plan adoption see 27–40% higher lifetime values compared to monthly-only. Offer annual plans at 2 months free:

| Plan | Monthly | Annual | Effective Monthly |
|------|---------|--------|-------------------|
| Practitioner Premium | $49/mo | $490/yr | $40.83/mo |
| Practitioner Featured | $129/mo | $1,290/yr | $107.50/mo |
| Center Premium | $79/mo | $790/yr | $65.83/mo |
| Center Featured | $199/mo | $1,990/yr | $165.83/mo |

Annual plans reduce churn dramatically (monthly churn in directories is 5–8%; annual plans reduce this near-zero during the term), give you upfront cash flow, and show practitioners you're confident they'll get value all year.

**3. You're missing a "middle" option for practitioners.**

Right now it's: Free ($0) → Premium ($49) → Featured ($129). That's a big gap. Consider whether a $19–$25/mo "Basic Plus" tier makes sense — something like unlimited bio + social links + 1 testimonial — that captures practitioners who want to look professional but aren't ready for the full analytics/booking suite. This is a decision for post-launch based on conversion data, not something to add now.

## The Kamaʻāina Question: For Life vs. 1 Year

This is the most consequential pricing decision you'll make at launch. Here's the honest math and the tradeoffs.

### Option A: Kamaʻāina for Life

**How it works now:** First 10 practitioners per tier get the discounted rate ($29/mo Premium, $99/mo Featured) locked in forever, as long as their subscription stays active.

**Pros:**

- **Maximum conversion power.** "For life" is an emotionally powerful offer. It taps into loss aversion — "if I don't sign up now, I lose this forever." That's stronger than any time-limited discount.
- **Creates founding-member identity.** These early subscribers become evangelists. "I got in at the Kamaʻāina rate" becomes a story they tell other practitioners.
- **Zero churn risk from price increases.** You'll never have to navigate the awkward "your rate is going up" conversation with your most loyal customers.
- **At $29/mo, it's still profitable.** Your marginal cost per subscriber is near zero (Supabase + Vercel costs are negligible). Even at $29/mo, 50 Kamaʻāina subscribers = $1,450/mo = $17,400/year in pure margin.

**Cons:**

- **Permanent revenue cap on your best customers.** As your directory grows and becomes more valuable (more traffic, better SEO, more visitors), early adopters get increasing value at a frozen price. A Featured spot worth $129/mo today might be worth $200/mo in 2 years — but your Kamaʻāina subscribers will still pay $99.
- **The math over 5 years.** 50 Kamaʻāina practitioners at $29/mo instead of $49/mo = $1,000/mo less = $12,000/year = $60,000 over 5 years in foregone revenue. Is the early traction worth that? Probably yes at your stage, but it adds up.
- **Sets a psychological anchor.** Once practitioners hear "$29/mo" in the market, that becomes the reference price in their heads. Full-price subscribers joining later may feel they're overpaying.

### Option B: Kamaʻāina for 1 Year (12 Months)

**How it works:** First 10 per tier get the discounted rate for 12 billing cycles, then automatically move to full price.

**Pros:**

- **Revenue scales with value.** After 12 months, your directory will be more established, have more traffic, and deliver more value. Full pricing reflects that increased value.
- **Cleaner long-term economics.** No permanent discount cohort dragging down ARPU as you grow.
- **Still a strong offer.** 12 months at 40% off is a $240 savings on Premium ($20/mo × 12). That's meaningful. And the framing "Lock in 40% off for your entire first year" is still compelling.

**Cons:**

- **Churn spike at month 13.** Data from SaaS grandfathering research shows you should expect 10–20% of discounted subscribers to cancel when the full rate kicks in. If you have 30 Kamaʻāina subscribers, you might lose 3–6 at the transition.
- **Weaker emotional hook.** "For your first year" is a deal. "For life" is an identity. The conversion rate difference is real — "for life" will outperform "1 year" in signups by an estimated 20–30%.
- **Requires communication management.** You need to email subscribers at month 10 and month 11 explaining the upcoming price change, showing them the value they've received ("your listing got 450 views and 67 contact clicks this year"), and framing the full price as "still a great deal." This is operational overhead.

### Option C (My Recommendation): Kamaʻāina for Life — But Cap It Tight

**Keep the "for life" framing, but limit it to a truly small number: 5 per tier, not 10.**

Here's why this is the best play for your stage:

1. **You need early traction more than you need revenue optimization.** You're pre-launch with zero paying subscribers. Converting your first 20–30 practitioners is existential. "For life" maximizes your conversion rate at the exact moment you need it most.

2. **5 per tier limits your exposure.** 5 Kamaʻāina Premium practitioners + 5 Kamaʻāina Featured = 10 total. At $29/mo and $99/mo respectively, that's $640/mo you're "leaving on the table" vs full price — only $7,680/year. That's a rounding error in the context of a business that should be doing $5K+/mo within a year.

3. **Scarcity makes it actually work.** "5 spots at this rate" is more believable and urgent than "10 spots." When someone sees "2 of 5 spots remaining," they act. At 10, it feels loose.

4. **After the 5 Kamaʻāina spots fill, transition to a 1-year launch discount.** Something like: "Kamaʻāina spots are full! But sign up before [date] and get 20% off your first 12 months." This gives you a two-stage launch: founding members (for life) → early adopters (1 year) → full price.

**The math on this hybrid approach:**

| Phase | Subscribers | Rate | Monthly Revenue |
|-------|------------|------|-----------------|
| Kamaʻāina (5 Premium + 5 Featured pracs) | 10 | $29–$99/mo | $640/mo |
| Launch discount (20% off, 1yr, ~20 pracs) | 20 | $39–$103/mo | ~$1,200/mo |
| Full price (ongoing) | 20+ | $49–$129/mo | ~$1,500+/mo |
| **Month 6 total** | ~50 | mixed | **~$3,300/mo** |

After year 1, the launch discount cohort moves to full price (with some churn), and your Kamaʻāina 10 stay as loyal ambassadors. By then, the directory has enough traffic and social proof that full-price conversions happen naturally.

## What You're Missing (Revised for Pre-Launch)

Since tier gates are already enforced, the gaps shift to launch-readiness and features that drive initial adoption:

### High Priority (Before or At Launch)

**1. "Claim Your Listing" flow for pipeline listings.**
As you scrub and publish listings, many of those businesses don't know they're in your directory. The moment a listing goes live, trigger an email: "Your practice is now on Hawaiʻi Wellness — claim your profile to update your details." Claimed listings convert to paid at 3–5× the rate of cold signups because the practitioner already sees they exist. This is your #1 growth engine.

**2. Replace mailto: on website packages with an intake form or booking link.**
This is a 1-day fix that unblocks your highest-margin revenue stream. Use Calendly, Cal.com, or even a simple Supabase-backed form.

**3. Display Google star ratings from pipeline data.**
You already have `rating` and `user_ratings_total` from your Google Maps pipeline. Displaying "4.8 ★ (142 reviews)" on listing cards is the single most powerful trust signal for directory visitors — and it makes the directory look more established at launch. Show this on all tiers.

**4. "Request Consultation" inquiry form.**
Add a "Contact this practitioner" button to every listing. For free tier, show the button but require the practitioner to be Premium to receive inquiries. This is the most natural upgrade trigger you can create — "Someone wants to book with you! Upgrade to Premium to see their message and respond."

### Medium Priority (Month 1–3 After Launch)

**5. Annual subscription option.**
Once you have monthly subscribers and can show them 2–3 months of value data, offer annual plans at 2 months free. This locks in revenue and reduces churn.

**6. Monthly performance email (automated).**
"Your listing got 83 views and 12 contact clicks this month." For paying subscribers, this proves ROI and reduces churn. For free users who don't get this data, it creates FOMO when they hear Premium users talking about their stats.

**7. Portfolio page for website packages.**
After your first 3–5 website builds, create a showcase page with screenshots and "View live site" links. This is when you raise website prices.

**8. Website + Directory subscription bundles.**
Once both products have traction, bundle them for higher ARPU.

### Lower Priority (Month 3–6)

**9. "Accepting New Clients" badge.** You have the field already (`accepts_new_clients`). Make it prominent — green badge on cards, filterable in search. Toggle available to Premium+ only.

**10. "Similar Practitioners" section on profiles.** Keeps visitors in your ecosystem, increases pageviews, and gives Featured practitioners additional exposure on other practitioners' profiles.

**11. Seasonal promotion slots.** One-time $50–$100 "Practitioner of the Month" or "Spring Wellness Spotlight" purchases. Lower commitment than a subscription — a good first purchase for fence-sitters.
