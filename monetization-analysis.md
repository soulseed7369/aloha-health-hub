## 1. Display Advertising

**What it is:** Ad placements on your directory pages, blog/article pages, and search results — served by an ad network.

**How it works for you:** Your health & wellness niche commands premium CPMs. US-based wellness traffic typically earns $15–30 CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions) through premium networks, and $5–10 CPM through Google AdSense. The key is qualifying for a premium network.

**Ad network tiers:**

- **Google AdSense** — No minimum traffic. Low RPM (~$5–10 for wellness). Available immediately.
- **Mediavine Journey** — Minimum 1,000 sessions/month. Better RPM (~$12–20). Good stepping stone.
- **Raptive** — Now requires 25,000 pageviews/month (lowered from 100K in Oct 2025), with 50% from tier-one countries. RPM of $15–30 for wellness.
- **Mediavine (full)** — Requires ~$5K/year in ad earnings to qualify for primary platform.

**Revenue estimates (monthly):**

| Scenario | Pageviews/mo | Network | RPM | Monthly Revenue |
|----------|-------------|---------|-----|-----------------|
| Conservative (3K–5K visits) | 6K–10K | AdSense | $7 | **$40–$70** |
| Moderate (8K–15K visits) | 16K–30K | Mediavine Journey | $15 | **$240–$450** |
| Strong (20K–40K visits) | 50K–100K | Raptive | $20 | **$1,000–$2,000** |

**Important:** Don't plaster ads on listing pages where practitioners are paying you for visibility — that cheapens their subscription. Focus ads on blog articles, the general directory browse pages, and informational content (e.g., "Best wellness retreats on the Big Island"). This protects your subscription value while monetizing the content traffic.

**6–12 month range: $500–$15,000 cumulative**

**Sponsorship types:**

## 5. Lead Generation Fees

**What it is:** Charging practitioners and centers a per-lead or per-booking fee for inquiries generated through your platform, separate from their subscription.

**Models:**

- **Pay-per-inquiry:** $5–$15 per qualified contact form submission or phone call routed through your platform.
- **Booking commission:** If you add a booking widget, take 5–10% of bookings made through your site.
- **Consultation matching:** Visitors describe what they need, you match them with 2–3 practitioners, charge the practitioner $20–$50 per match.

**Why this works here:** Wellness services are high-value transactions. A single massage session is $80–$200+, a retreat is $500–$5,000+. Practitioners will gladly pay $10–$15 for a qualified lead.

**Revenue estimates (monthly, at maturity):**

| Scenario | Leads/mo | Fee/lead | Monthly Revenue |
|----------|---------|----------|-----------------|
| Conservative | 20–40 | $10 | **$200–$400** |
| Moderate | 60–120 | $12 | **$720–$1,440** |
| Strong | 200–400 | $15 | **$3,000–$6,000** |

**Implementation note:** This requires adding contact tracking (e.g., a "Request Appointment" form or trackable phone numbers). You already have the infrastructure in your listings — adding a tracked inquiry form is a natural extension.

**6–12 month range: $1,200–$45,000 cumulative**

## 7. Email Newsletter Monetization

**What it is:** Building an email list of wellness-interested visitors and monetizing it through a mix of sponsored placements, affiliate offers, and driving traffic back to monetized pages.

**Growth path:**

- 0–6 months: Build list to 500–2,000 subscribers using a free lead magnet (e.g., "Top 10 Hidden Wellness Gems in Hawaiʻi").
- 6–12 months: Scale to 2,000–5,000+ with consistent weekly/biweekly content.

**Monetization methods:**

- Sponsored slots: $50–$200/issue once list hits 1K+
- Affiliate offers: Retreat deals, wellness products — 2–5% of list clicks through, 2–3% of those convert
- Drive traffic: Send readers to articles with display ads and affiliate links

## Recommended Priority Order

Given your moderate effort appetite, here's the order I'd tackle these:

**Month 1–2: Quick wins**
1. **Retreat affiliates** — You already have a retreats page. Add affiliate links to Tripaneer, BookRetreats, and Viator. Minimal dev work, immediate earning potential.
2. **Display ads** — Set up AdSense on blog articles immediately. Apply to Mediavine Journey once you hit 1K sessions/month. Passive income from day one.
3. **Product affiliates** — Add Amazon/iHerb links to 3–5 curated content pieces. Low effort, compounds over time.

**Month 3–4: Build the engine**
4. **Email newsletter** — Add a lead magnet + signup form. Start weekly/biweekly sends. This becomes the distribution channel for everything else.
5. **Sponsored content** — Once you have traffic data to show, reach out to 5–10 local resorts, spas, and wellness brands about sponsored features.

**Month 5–6: Scale what works**
6. **Lead generation** — Add tracked inquiry forms to listings. Test with a few premium practitioners first, then roll out as a feature.
7. **Digital products** — Create your first guide based on what content performs best.

## Key Assumptions & Caveats

- Revenue ramps up — month 1 will be much lower than month 12. The cumulative figures reflect this curve.
- Conversion rates assume a wellness-intent audience (higher than general web traffic).
- Sponsored content rates depend heavily on your ability to show traffic data and audience quality to potential sponsors.
- Lead gen requires product changes to your site (tracked forms, phone routing).
- These estimates are based on industry benchmarks for niche directory and wellness sites. Actual results depend on traffic quality, SEO execution, and content strategy.
- The Hawaiʻi wellness niche is premium — advertisers and sponsors pay more for this audience than generic wellness traffic because it overlaps with high-income travel intent.
